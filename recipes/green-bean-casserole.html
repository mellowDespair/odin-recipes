Green Bean Casserole

A picture of Green Bean Casserole

Green bean casserole is a classic American dish that has become a staple on Thanksgiving and holiday tables across the country. This iconic casserole typically features fresh green beans, creamy mushroom soup, and crispy fried onions. The dish's appeal lies in its comforting combination of textures and flavors. The green beans provide a crisp and vibrant element, contrasting with the rich and savory mushroom soup. The addition of crispy fried onions on top adds a delightful crunch and a burst of flavor. This casserole not only serves as a delicious side dish but also evokes a sense of nostalgia, as many people associate it with family gatherings and festive celebrations.

One of the great things about green bean casserole is its versatility. While the classic recipe remains a favorite, there are countless variations and creative twists to suit different tastes and preferences. Some people opt for homemade mushroom sauce instead of canned soup, adding depth and authenticity to the flavor profile. Others experiment with alternative toppings like crumbled bacon or toasted almonds for a unique twist. Whether served as a side dish for a holiday feast or as a comforting addition to a weeknight dinner, green bean casserole continues to be a beloved and customizable dish that brings people together around the dining table.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups green beans, trimmed and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 can (10.5 ounces) condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 1/3 cups fried onions (French-fried onions)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Boil or steam the green beans until they are tender-crisp. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix together the cream of mushroom soup, milk, soy sauce, and black pepper.
  4. Add the cooked green beans to the soup mixture and stir until well coated.
  5. Transfer the green bean mixture to a greased baking dish.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 25 minutes, or until the casserole is hot and bubbly.
  7. Remove the casserole from the oven and sprinkle the fried onions evenly over the top.
  8. Return the casserole to the oven and bake for an additional 5 minutes, or until the onions are golden brown.
  9. Serve the green bean casserole warm and enjoy!
